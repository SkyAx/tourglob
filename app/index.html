History Of Belarus

Belarus is the country between East and West, the Baltics and the Black sea, part of the trade route “from the Varangians to the Greeks”. Our country was part of a number of statehoods including the Principality of Polotsk, the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, the Kingdom of Poland and the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, the Russian Empire, the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics. After the dissolution of the USSR in 1991, Belarus began its history as an independent state and in 1994 the new Constitution was adopted as well as the first presidential election held. Being in the centre of Europe, Belarus was integrated in the common course of history of the latter but managed to develop its own peculiarities which are worth of meticulous study. This is the country to discover.

ENJOY THE FREE ENTRY TO BELARUS!

Belarus established visa-free regime for the citizens of 80 states for up to 5 days.

Entry requirements:

  • - arrival and departure only via “Minsk National Airport”*
  • - a valid passport or another substituting document for traveling abroad;
  • - financial means: at least 25 Euro (or equal amount in dollars or Belarusian rubles) for each day of stay;
  • - medical insurance with coverage for at least 10,000 Euros valid on the territory of Belarus.
*The given order of visa-free movement through the airport does not extend to persons coming to Belarus by plane from the Russian Federation, as well as to those who intend to fly to the airports of the Russian Federation  The Decree also does not apply to foreigners paying official visits. Diplomatic, official, special and other passports equal to them are not covered by the above Decree.

Customs

It is allowed for the tourists arriving by plane to bring personal luggage up to 50 kg equivalent to 10 000 euros. In case of extra weight the items will be subject to duty at 30% and not less than 4 euros per kilo. Cash over 10 000 USD shall be declared. People over the age of 18 are allowed to bring in 3 litres of alcoholic drinks including beer, 200 cigarettes or 50 cigars or 250g tobacco. Firearms, military equipment, narcotics, poisons, radioactive and explosive substances are not allowed.

Insurance

It is obligatory to possess an insurance to enter the countrywith coverage for at least 10,000 Euros.

Currency

The local currency of Belarus is the Belarusian ruble available in banknotes and coins. You will be able to change money when you arrive in a large number of exchange offices in all the cities of Belarus or withdraw it from ATMs located in any bank, shopping mall or supermarket. The most commonly accepted currencies are:

  • US dollars
  • Euros
  • Russian rubles

Local Rules

From the very first minute in the country you will see how clean, well maintained and organised Belarus is. People don't litter, cross the streets under the green light, smoking and alcohol drinking in stations, shops, parks and other public places are forbidden. Children under 16 can be outside till 23:00, after this time they should be accompanied by adults. The entrance to the clubs is allowed for full age people after 18 years.

Transportation

The public transportation is well developed including buses, trams, trolley buses, vans and taxis. There is also underground in Minsk, the capital of the Republic of Belarus. It is possible to rent a car for a foreigner in Belarus holding an International Driving Permit. The driving in Belarus is right-side.

Agroecotourism

You will feel as close to nature as never before while visiting a vast array of complexes with crystal clear air, mirror-like rivers and lakes where all the beauty of  the surrounding landscape is reflected.  This can be a heaven for the families with children to relax and get acquainted with the history and traditions, for nature lovers, hunters, fishers and everyone who wants to escape to peace and quiet.

Wellness and SPA

Belarus boasts a huge variety of facilities for regeneration and recuperation where you can combine relaxation with high-quality treatment pursuant to best diagnosis and medical bases. The majority of health centres are located in the forests, on the banks of rivers and lakes that creates a special microclimate. According to the ranking of the National Geographic Traveler Awards 2016  Belarus entered the top 5 countries for health improvement. Allocate some time to dedicate to yourself!

Cultural heritage

Belarus is located at the geopolitical as well as historic crossroads of Europe and this strategic position predetermined our country to become the path of huge military conquests and deep versatile cultural roots during its history counting thousands of years. You will find UNESCO cultural heritage sites as well as a great variety of museums of all sorts.

Nightlife

You will have the opportunity to enjoy performance on stage and on screen, let out your inner singer in karaoke bars, try your luck in casinos. Who said nights were for sleep?
